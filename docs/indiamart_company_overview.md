# indiamart_company_overview.md

## Purpose

This document summarizes IndiaMART as a company and marketplace so a sales-call-preparation solution can remain grounded in IndiaMART's business context. It avoids generic AI or chatbot design and focuses only on IndiaMART-specific marketplace, seller, buyer, lead, and paid-service context.

## Source-quality note

- Public facts are taken mainly from IndiaMART investor material, IndiaMART app listings, public regulatory documents, and reliable market sources.
- Attachment-derived facts are taken from user-provided IndiaMART training/product decks and the user-provided business-model image.
- Inferences are marked clearly where a claim is a practical interpretation rather than a published IndiaMART rule.

## What IndiaMART does

IndiaMART is an Indian online B2B marketplace that connects business buyers with suppliers of business products and services. The marketplace supports product discovery, supplier discovery, enquiry generation, buyer-seller communication, and seller-side lead handling.

Public IndiaMART investor material describes the platform as a buyer-supplier matching network where buyers submit requirements and suppliers receive and manage relevant opportunities. IndiaMART's Q3 FY26 investor presentation also frames the core marketplace around discovery, RFQ and BuyLead matching, lead management, paid supplier subscriptions, priority listing, and supplier web storefronts.

From the user-provided marketplace overview deck, the simplest business model is:

| Side | Marketplace action |
|---|---|
| Buyers | Post enquiries or buying requirements for products |
| Suppliers | Provide quotations and respond to buyer requirements |
| IndiaMART | Enables buyer-seller discovery, listing, contact, lead delivery, and paid visibility services |

Source: User-provided attachment, `Marketplace Overview V1.0`, slide 3.

## Role in Indian B2B commerce

IndiaMART acts as a large horizontal B2B marketplace for Indian businesses. It supports discovery across a broad range of industrial, commercial, manufacturing, trading, wholesale, and service categories.

Public IndiaMART investor material reports the following operating scale in the Q3 FY26 presentation:

|---|---:|---|

| Indian supplier storefronts | 8.7 million | Q3 FY26 |

| Employees | 6,353 | Q3 FY26 |

Public source: IndiaMART Q3 FY26 investor presentation.

The same public investor presentation mentions IndiaMART's marketplace breadth across thousands of categories and dozens of industries. This matters for salespeople because a seller call is rarely just a generic "sell online" conversation. It is usually tied to a seller's products, categories, location, lead flow, catalogue health, and subscription status.

## Buyer-seller marketplace model

The IndiaMART marketplace can be understood as a two-sided discovery and lead-generation model.

| Component | IndiaMART-specific meaning |
|---|---|
| Buyer discovery | Buyers search or browse products, suppliers, and categories on IndiaMART web, mobile site, and mobile app. |
| Supplier discovery | Sellers create free or paid catalogues and product listings so buyers can find them. |
| Enquiry and contact | Buyers can contact suppliers through calls, contact forms, Get Best Price flows, WhatsApp-enabled interactions, or buying requirement forms, depending on surface and supplier setup. |
| BuyLead / RFQ matching | Buyers submit requirements. IndiaMART matches or surfaces these requirements to relevant suppliers. Public investor material describes AI-enabled enrichment and matchmaking for RFQ/BuyLead flows. |
| Supplier lead handling | Sellers use seller-facing systems such as Seller.IndiaMART.com and Lead Manager to reply, label, follow up, quote, and manage buyer leads. |
| Paid seller services | IndiaMART monetizes through subscriptions and paid service packages that can include priority listing, BuyLead credits, catalogue services, trust signals, web storefronts, and seller tools. |

## Major public milestones and product evolution

Public IndiaMART sources and securities documents indicate that IndiaMART's corporate journey includes the following broad milestones:

| Year or period | Publicly visible milestone or evolution |
|---|---|
| 1999 | IndiaMART InterMESH Limited was incorporated, according to its public DRHP. |
| 2000s | IndiaMART built a B2B supplier discovery and listing business around business products and services. |
| 2003 | Public secondary market sources and IndiaMART training material refer to TrustSEAL as an early trust/verification product. Use this as historical context only unless confirmed from a primary IndiaMART annual report. |
| 2019 | IndiaMART listed on Indian stock exchanges through an IPO. |
| 2020s | IndiaMART investor material shows continued growth in buyers, suppliers, product listings, paid suppliers, and product/technology capabilities. |
| 2022 onward | IndiaMART increased focus on business enablement and accounting through subsidiaries and investments, including Busy Infotech and Livekeeping, as shown in investor presentations. |
| 2023 to 2025 | Public investor material lists features such as price discovery, AI chatbot, voice and multilingual search, photo search, and AI voice agents as part of continuing product evolution. |

## Revenue and business model at a high level

IndiaMART's business model is best understood as a freemium B2B marketplace monetized mainly from suppliers.

| Model layer | What happens |
|---|---|
| Free supplier presence | Suppliers can create free catalogues or listings with business and product details. |
| Paid subscriptions | Suppliers can pay for services that improve visibility, trust, catalogue quality, lead access, and communication capabilities. |
| BuyLead/RFQ credits | Suppliers can use BuyLead balance/credits to consume buyer requirements. Public investor material describes RFQ selection through paid quota and IndiaMART training decks describe weekly and daily BuyLead allocations. |
| Priority listing and visibility | Paid services can improve positioning in listing-order contexts, though public search relevance may use separate logic. |
| Seller engagement tools | Lead Manager, buyer profile details, PNS, and communication tools support conversion and retention. |

Important caveat: exact internal ranking, matching, and renewal algorithms are not public. The user-provided decks give operational training context, but the production logic may vary by product, time, geography, category, and seller history.

## Important IndiaMART terminology

| Term | Short meaning |
|---|---|
| Buyer | Business user searching for products/services or posting a buying requirement. |
| Supplier / Seller | Business listed on IndiaMART to receive enquiries, calls, and leads. |
| Enquiry | Buyer-initiated contact or requirement sent to a supplier. |
| BuyLead / BL | A buyer requirement available for suppliers to consume through BuyLead balance or paid lead access. |
| Bizfeed | User-provided deck term for buyer product or catalogue view signals when no direct enquiry is posted. |
| Catalogue | Seller web/storefront page with company and product details. |
| Product listing | Individual product/service page or card with name, image, price, specs, category, city, and supplier details. |
| MCAT | Internal-observed term for marketplace category or category node used for listing, lead matching, and paid product configuration. |
| Lead Manager / LMS | Seller-side lead management area used to reply, label, follow up, quote, and manage buyers. |
| PNS | Preferred Number Service, a call-routing setup described in user-provided MDC material. |
| TrustSEAL | Verification and trust-signalling service with logo, stamp, certificate, and trust report. |
| MDC | Mini Dynamic Catalog, a paid catalogue service. |
| Maximiser | Paid catalogue/online marketing package with own domain, design options, TrustSEAL, and BuyLead allocation. |
| Star / Leader / Industry Leader | Listing order or higher visibility paid service tiers observed in internal material. |
| IMA | IndiaMART Advantage Program, observed in user-provided and public proposal-style sources as tied to BuyLead allocation. |

## Relevance for sales-call-preparation

For IndiaMART salespeople, seller-call context should not be generic. A good domain-grounded prep should include:

| Sales-call context | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Seller's category and MCATs | Category determines buyer demand, listing competition, package relevance, and whether Star/Leader style products may apply. |
| Location and preferred districts | Some paid services and lead preference logic are location-sensitive. |
| Current catalogue health | Poor images, unclear names, missing prices, weak product groups, or wrong categories can hurt seller discovery and conversion. |
| Lead flow | The salesperson should know whether the seller gets enquiries, BuyLeads, calls, catalogue views, and callbacks. |
| Response behaviour | Quick calling, emails, Lead Manager follow-up, labels, quotations, and WhatsApp engagement can affect conversion. |
| Trust and credibility | TrustSEAL and verified information are relevant when sellers complain about buyer trust or conversion. |
| Package fit | MDC, TrustSEAL, Maximiser, Star, Leader, IM Insta, and export services solve different seller needs. |
| Renewal/servicing context | Internal training emphasizes that service experience, ownership, tone, and follow-through are important for renewals. |
