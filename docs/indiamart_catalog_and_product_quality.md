# indiamart_catalog_and_product_quality.md

## Purpose

This document explains IndiaMART catalogue, product listing, and product-quality context for sales-call preparation. It focuses on why product names, categories, images, price, specifications, city, and descriptions matter for seller visibility and lead conversion.

## Source-quality note

- Public facts come from IndiaMART investor material, app listings, and publicly indexed IndiaMART catalogue/proposal examples.
- Attachment-derived facts come from user-provided Marketplace, MDC, Maximiser, TrustSEAL, and Product Overview decks.
- Exact internal product-quality rules are not public. Operational relevance is clearly separated from public facts.

## What product listings look like on IndiaMART

A typical IndiaMART product listing or supplier catalogue is intended to help a business buyer answer:

| Buyer question | Listing element that helps |
|---|---|
| What is this product/service? | Product name and category. |
| Does it match my requirement? | Specifications, description, use-case, size, material, capacity, model, industry tags. |
| Can I trust this seller? | Business profile, TrustSEAL, verified details, GST, reviews/ratings, years in business, response rate. |
| Where is the seller located? | City, state, address, supplier location. |
| Can I contact quickly? | Call, Contact Supplier, Get Best Price, WhatsApp, PNS, Lead Manager replies. |
| Is this a serious supplier? | Images, price, product range, catalogue completeness, certifications, business type. |

Public investor material highlights product specifications, prices, photos/videos, reviews/ratings, buyer-supplier negotiations, customer history/reminders, quotations, invoicing, and conversation as marketplace and commerce-enablement elements.

## Catalogue types in user-provided IndiaMART context

The user-provided Product Overview and training decks distinguish several catalogue contexts:

| Catalogue/service type | Attachment-derived identification |
|---|---|
| Free Catalog Page | IndiaMART logo and search option appear on the header; blue strip at top; IndiaMART info in footer. |
| Mini Dynamic Catalog / MDC | No IndiaMART header/footer, no blue strip, single templated, runs on subdomain. |
| Maximiser | No IndiaMART header/footer, no blue strip, multiple templates, runs on own domain. |
| TrustSEAL catalog context | TrustSEAL logo, stamp, certificate, and trust report provide credibility/visibility elements. |

Sources: User-provided `Product Overview & Servicing`, slides 5 to 7; `MDC_3.0`, slides 3 to 9; `Maximiser_3.0`, slides 6 to 11; `TrustSEAL_3.0`, slides 6 to 11.

## Why product names matter

The user-provided MDC deck's NSD checklist refers to "specific keywords" and "2/3 words product name" as part of product setup. This implies that product names should be short, specific, and buyer-search-aligned.

### Good listing name characteristics

| Characteristic | Example style |
|---|---|
| Specific | "Stainless Steel Storage Tank" rather than "Tank". |
| Buyer-searchable | Uses words the buyer would type. |
| Category-aligned | Matches the actual MCAT/category. |
| Not stuffed | Avoids unreadable keyword strings. |
| Not vague | Avoids "Best Quality Product", "New Item", "Machine", or "Service". |

Source: User-provided attachment, `MDC_3.0`, slide 18.

## Why categories and MCATs matter

IndiaMART's marketplace uses categories for discovery, listing, and paid service configuration. The user-provided decks use MCAT/category language heavily in listing order, Star/Leader Pro, and product management contexts.

| Category/MCAT role | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Buyer search and filtering | Buyers search and filter by category and related terms. |
| Listing order | User-provided Marketplace deck describes DIR MCAT listing order. |
| Paid services | Star/Leader Pro product construct depends on selected categories/MCATs. |
| Lead matching | BuyLead/RFQ matching depends on buyer requirement and supplier relevance. |
| Preferred locations | Star/Leader Pro combines category and location/district logic. |
| Product quality | Wrong category can create irrelevant leads and poor buyer experience. |

Source: User-provided `Marketplace Overview V1.0`, slides 21, 25 to 27; `IM Star Pro - IM Leader Pro`, slides 4 to 8, 11, 19 to 24.

Public caveat: exact MCAT taxonomy and internal category-quality rules are not publicly documented in the sources reviewed.

## Why images matter

The user-provided Marketplace deck says that within a customer-type group in DIR MCAT listing, ranking by product enrichment status follows:

1. Suppliers having products with price.
2. Suppliers having products with image.
3. Suppliers having products without image.

Source: User-provided attachment, `Marketplace Overview V1.0`, slide 26.

This does not mean image alone guarantees top search position. The same deck says IndiaMART Search does not follow listing order and is based on relevancy. Source: `Marketplace Overview V1.0`, slide 27.

Sales-call use:

| Seller issue | Product-image action |
|---|---|
| Products have no photos | Add clear real product images. |
| Photos are generic or unclear | Replace with sharp, product-specific images. |
| Buyer asks too many basic questions | Add images that show product, size, material, and application. |
| Seller has many duplicate-looking products | Use distinct images and product names to reduce confusion. |

## Why price matters

The user-provided Marketplace deck places products with price first in product enrichment status within listing-order groups. Price helps B2B buyers assess fit, shortlist sellers, and decide whether to contact.

Operational relevance:

| Price issue | Likely impact |
|---|---|
| No price on product | Buyer may skip if looking for quick comparison. |
| Wrong price | Leads become low-quality or negotiations fail. |
| Price too vague | Buyer cannot judge whether supplier fits budget. |
| Price outdated | Seller may get disputes or conversion loss. |

Caveat: many B2B products are customized or quote-based, so price may be range-based or "as per specification". The key point is clarity, not necessarily fixed ecommerce pricing.

## Why specifications and descriptions matter

Public IndiaMART investor material explicitly refers to product specifications and prices as discovery content. In B2B buying, specifications reduce ambiguity.

Useful specification fields depend on category, for example:

| Category type | Useful specs |
|---|---|
| Machinery | Capacity, automation grade, power, material, application, dimensions, warranty. |
| Chemicals | Grade, purity, packaging, use, safety/compliance, MOQ. |
| Construction materials | Size, thickness, grade, finish, application, delivery area. |
| Apparel/textiles | Fabric, GSM, size, color, MOQ, customization. |
| Industrial components | Material, tolerance, standard, model, compatibility. |
| Services | Service area, process, turnaround time, certifications, industries served. |

Inferred operational relevance: incomplete specifications can create irrelevant enquiries because buyers must guess product fit. Complete specs help seller sales teams avoid repetitive qualification questions.

## Why city/location matters

The user-provided Marketplace deck says buyers can connect with suppliers using filters such as location, category, and brands. Star/Leader Pro training also uses preferred locations and preferred districts.

| Location element | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| City | Buyers often prefer nearby suppliers for logistics, visits, or faster delivery. |
| State/region | Relevant for industrial supply chains and local distribution. |
| Preferred districts | Star/Leader Pro context uses dynamic preferred districts based on BuyLead consumption behaviour. |
| All India page | Preferred Star/Leader variants in user-provided material include broader location presence. |
| Wrong location | May generate leads that seller cannot serve, hurting conversion. |

Sources: User-provided `Marketplace Overview V1.0`, slide 21; `IM Star Pro - IM Leader Pro`, slides 7, 8, 11, 23, and 24.

## Product rejection, unclear names, duplicate products, and incorrect products

No official public IndiaMART product-quality rulebook was found in the reviewed public sources. The following are operationally relevant inferences from marketplace logic and user-provided training:

| Quality issue | Why it matters | Call-prep angle |
|---|---|---|
| Incorrect category | Can produce wrong buyer matches and poor search relevance. | Ask seller what exact product and buyer segment they serve. |
| Unclear product name | Buyer cannot tell what is being sold. | Rename with specific buyer-search phrase. |
| Duplicate products | Clutters catalogue and splits relevance. | Merge or differentiate by model, size, material, grade, use case. |
| Missing image | Lower enrichment and weak buyer trust. | Add real product images. |
| Missing price | Buyer cannot compare or shortlist. | Add price or price range where feasible. |
| Incomplete specs | Buyer must call for basic details, causing friction. | Add category-specific specs. |
| Wrong city | Buyer lead may not be serviceable. | Update business and product-level geography. |
| Outdated product | Seller receives irrelevant enquiries. | Remove, archive, or update. |
| Poor trust profile | Buyer may hesitate. | Verify details and consider TrustSEAL where eligible. |

## Catalogue enrichment checklist for seller calls

Based on user-provided decks, a salesperson can prepare a catalogue-quality call with this checklist:

| Checklist area | Questions to prepare |
|---|---|
| Product coverage | Are the seller's main revenue products listed? Are fast-selling products missing? |
| Product name | Are names specific, short, and buyer-searchable? |
| Category mapping | Are products mapped to the correct MCAT/category? |
| Price | Is price present where possible? Is it updated? |
| Images | Do products have clear and distinct images? |
| Specs | Are important specs filled for the category? |
| Product groups | Are products logically grouped? |
| City/location | Is location accurate and aligned with seller serviceable areas? |
| Trust info | Are GST, legal status, profile, certifications, and TrustSEAL context correct? |
| Contact readiness | Are PNS/contact numbers and Lead Manager workflows working? |
| Lead handling | Is seller consuming fresh leads and following up? |

## How poor catalogue quality could affect seller outcomes

| Poor catalogue issue | Possible seller outcome |
|---|---|
| Weak product names | Buyers may not discover or understand the listing. |
| Wrong category | Buyer matches become irrelevant. |
| No image or poor image | Lower trust and weaker product evaluation. |
| Missing price/specs | Buyer may contact competitors with clearer details. |
| Thin catalogue | Seller may look less established. |
| Outdated location/contact | Missed calls and failed callbacks. |
| Duplicate/incorrect products | Confused buyer flow and poor lead quality. |
| No trust signals | Buyer hesitation, especially for high-value B2B orders. |
| Slow response | Competitors may convert the buyer first. |
